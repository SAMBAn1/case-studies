THE READ

Before growth tactics, get the story straight.

I'm treating this as a working assumption, because the current product page doesn't make the story obvious in the first few seconds — and if I can't get it in five seconds, neither can a buyer scanning past it.

WHO IT'S FOR

Wholesalers, distributors, manufacturers and B2B brands already selling through Shopify or Adobe Commerce who need better ordering, account management, and approvals.

WHAT PAIN IT SOLVES

Too many phone, email, and rep-led orders. Pricing nobody can manage cleanly. Buyers who can't self-serve. ERP sync that's broken, manual, or missing entirely.

WHY SOMEONE BUYS IT

The core B2B features, without paying for a much larger platform move — plus ERP-linked workflows most alternatives can't touch.

PLAIN-ENGLISH POSITIONING LINE

"This B2B Commerce Portal helps a seller turn a regular store into a usable B2B ordering portal — customer pricing, approvals, quick order flows, and ERP-aware operations."

THE GAP

Why demand leaks before it ever reaches sales.

Before spending hard on outreach, fix the product story and the website journey — otherwise every dollar of outreach just fills a leaky top of funnel.

WHAT'S LEAKING DEMAND TODAY

  • The headline sells platform names, not the buyer's job to be done
  • The feature area reads like a PRD, not a product story
  • No quick visual proof of what problems actually get solved
  • The page creates interest, but never urgency

HIGHEST-IMPACT FIXES

  • Make the CTA go somewhere useful — calendar booking, instant demo, or a short form with a real next step
  • Show the product in motion: quick order, account roles, approvals, quotes, checkout with terms
  • Turn every major feature into a 20–45 second mini demo, on the site and on YouTube

CONTENT I'D LAUNCH FAST

3 short YouTube demos 3 customer story clips 1 comparison page vs. Shopify Plus / Adobe B2B LinkedIn posts with screenshots, not text walls A podcast series with eCommerce operators & ERP partners

THE FIRST WEDGE

Don't chase the whole market. Start where the pain explains itself.

One narrow customer group, chosen because the pain is easiest to explain and easiest to demo — not because it's the biggest addressable market on a slide.

IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE

US mid-market wholesalers, distributors & manufacturers that:

Already run Shopify or Adobe Commerce Use NetSuite, Dynamics, or SAP B1 Still rely on manual order handling Need approvals, customer pricing & rep-assisted ordering

HOW TO FIND THEM

  • Start with the company's existing service and integration customers
  • Ask agency and ERP implementation partners for accounts where B2B ordering is still messy
  • Build LinkedIn lists by title: VP eCommerce, Ops Director, IT Director, Sales Ops
  • Use demo signups, webinar signups, and comparison-page traffic as warm intent lists

WHO OWNS OUTREACH

  • Sales owns day-to-day outbound and follow-ups
  • Founder / CEO joins the first big calls to speed trust and learning
  • PM owns the message, the demo flow, and objection handling
  • Partners bring warm intros where B2B and ERP show up together

ACQUISITION MOTION

The road to 100, in three phases.

Win a small segment first, then repeat the exact same story with better proof each time.

0 → 10

First

  • Warm accounts first: existing relationships, partner intros, named target accounts
  • A tight demo around 3 moments only — bulk order, customer pricing, ERP-aware checkout
  • A short discovery + implementation plan, instead of a vague "book a demo"

10 → 40

Repeat

  • Turn early wins into proof: two case studies, one webinar, one ROI story
  • Simple outbound by segment — distributors, manufacturers, B2B brands
  • Publish the same proof everywhere: site, YouTube, LinkedIn, partner pages

40 → 100

Scale

  • Double down on the best verticals and best ERP combinations
  • Launch partner co-selling with agencies and ERP consultants
  • A lighter marketplace/connector entry point — only if it clearly feeds the core product

"Sell B2B online with approvals, pricing, and ERP-aware workflows — without jumping to a much larger platform bill."

WHY THIS CAN WORK

The unfair advantage: this isn't starting from zero.

Most new product launches don't have this. This one already does.

Existing credibility in commerce and ERP work
A services engine that can create product-qualified opportunities
Reference brands on the site and real integration experience
A natural story around storefront + ERP + operations
